A Beginning and an End

A poem by Jared Whitney

A girl is kneeling down on top of a mountain,
Look upon her eye: Teary,
and dark as the night that
surrounds her,
But a glimmer of hope,
of determination in it.

A phoenix appears,
Burning red like fire inside,
But seeming by the rainy storm
To be but warm as the hearth:

Its wings envelop the broken girl.

Slip back down below, into the forest.
Green, the sun shines.
It is springtime, and the flowers are in bloom:
White lilies...

They seem so innocent, so pure,
But all the same remind the boy
who trundles through them
with his little sister,
of death.

Too young to understand,
she gazes up at him
with her rows of gnashing teeth,
her eyes charred black
like the hell that she knows so well.

With a sad smile, the two walk on.

On a long winding road,
a lone figure walks.
Her face covered by a cloak,
she barely glances up
when others pass her by.

As darkness falls,
she slows her pace;
above her circles a crow:
"You lied, you lied!"...
With shame she quickly turns away,
the world knows her treachery.

But the sounds of the spirits
whisper softly to her as always:
because of them she is never truly alone.

As she walks up onto the top of the mountain,
she kneels and bows her head,
the warmth of the hearth
surrounds her...
and for once she is able to smile.

When she opens her eyes,
they reflect an image of the sun
slipping over the horizon,
and she quits the company of man
for the last time.
